Nandi is a region located in western Kenya that experiences two rainy seasons and two dry seasons. The long rainy season occurs from March to May, while the short rainy season occurs from October to December. The long dry season occurs from June to September, and the short dry season occurs from January to February.
  • Long rainy season (March to May):

    • This is the best time to plant most crops because there is plenty of water available. Some of the crops that thrive during this season include maize, beans, peas, cowpeas, millet, and sorghum.

  • Short rainy season (October to December):

    • This season is characterized by erratic rainfall, and therefore, it's recommended to plant crops that are drought-tolerant. Some of the crops that can be planted during this season include sweet potatoes, cassava, yams, and some varieties of beans.

  • Long dry season (June to September):

    • This is a challenging season for farmers as there is little or no rainfall, and therefore, it's recommended to plant crops that are drought-resistant. Some of the crops that can be planted during this season include cowpeas, pigeon peas, green grams, and sorghum.

  • Short dry season (January to February):

    • This is a short season, and it's recommended to plant fast-growing crops that can be harvested within a short time. Some of the crops that can be planted during this season include kales, spinach, and onions.
