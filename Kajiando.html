Kajiado County is located in Kenya, and its climate is predominantly semi-arid. The county experiences two major seasons - the dry season and the wet season. The dry season runs from June to October, while the wet season runs from November to May.

Here are some crops that can be planted in different seasons based on Kajiado County's weather conditions:

  1. Dry Season (June-October):

    2. During the dry season, temperatures can be very high, and rainfall is minimal. Therefore, crops that are resistant to drought and can thrive in dry conditions are ideal. Some crops that can be planted during the dry season in Kajiado County include:

    • Maize: Maize is a staple food crop in Kenya and can be planted during the dry season in Kajiado County. However, it requires irrigation to ensure it gets enough water to survive.
    • Sorghum: Sorghum is a drought-tolerant crop that can withstand high temperatures and minimal rainfall. It can be planted during the dry season and is commonly grown for its grain, which is used to make flour.
    • Cowpeas: Cowpeas are a type of legume that can be planted during the dry season in Kajiado County. They are drought-tolerant and can grow well in sandy soils.
