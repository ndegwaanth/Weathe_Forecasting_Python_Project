Elgeyo-Marakwet county is located in the Rift Valley region of Kenya and experiences two distinct rainy seasons, with the long rains occurring from March to May and the short rains from October to December. The county also experiences two dry seasons, from December to March and from June to September.

Based on the different weather seasons, the following crops can be planted in Elgeyo-Marakwet county:

  • Long rains season (March to May):

    • During this season, crops that require a lot of water can be planted. Some of the crops that can be planted include maize, beans, peas, potatoes, and vegetables such as kales, spinach, and cabbages. Farmers can also plant fruit trees such as avocados, mangoes, and passion fruits.

  • Dry season (June to September):

    • During this season, water is scarce, and therefore, farmers need to plant crops that require minimal water. Some of the crops that can be planted during this season include sorghum, millet, and cowpeas. Farmers can also plant drought-tolerant vegetables such as amaranth, cowpea leaves, and spider plant.

  • Short rains season (October to December):

    • During this season, farmers can plant crops that can withstand heavy rainfall. Some of the crops that can be planted during this season include maize, beans, cowpeas, and green grams. Farmers can also plant vegetables such as spinach, kales, and cabbages.

It is essential for farmers in Elgeyo-Marakwet county to consider the different weather seasons when planning their crops. By planting crops that are suitable for each season, farmers can maximize their yields and ensure food security for their families and the community.
